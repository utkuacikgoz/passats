Questions you're probably thinking

What is an ATS score and why should I care?

An ATS (Applicant Tracking System) score is the number that decides whether your resume reaches a human or gets auto-rejected into the void. Most companies — from startups to Fortune 500 — use ATS software to filter candidates before a recruiter ever opens your file. If your score is low, it doesn't matter how qualified you are. You're invisible.

How is this different from free ATS checkers?

Most free ATS resume checkers give you a vague score and then upsell you on a $40/month subscription or a resume rewrite service. PassATS gives you the full report — ATS compatibility score, keyword gap analysis, formatting audit, and prioritized fixes — for $2.99. No account. No recurring charge. No data harvesting. That's it.

Why am I applying to jobs and hearing nothing back?

Probably because a robot rejected you. Most resumes never reach a human reviewer. The ATS scans for specific keywords, checks formatting compatibility, and makes a pass/fail decision in milliseconds. If your resume uses tables, columns, graphics, or creative headers, the system literally can't read half of it. You're getting filtered out before anyone sees your experience.

What file formats work?

PDF, DOC, and DOCX — up to 5 MB. PDF is best. It's what ATS platforms handle most consistently. Avoid image-based PDFs (like scanned documents) — if a human can't select the text, neither can the ATS.

What happens to my resume after I upload it?

Nothing. It's processed in real time, analyzed, and immediately discarded. We don't store it, we don't train on it, we don't sell it. Payment goes through Stripe — we never see your card details. Your data is yours.
